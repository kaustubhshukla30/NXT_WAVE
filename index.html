The read on the category

Don't out-place the field. Open a category.

Every serious India player runs the same motion: train, then push graduates into generic software roles through a hiring-partner network, with a headline placement rate as the marketing. That motion is now commoditized and cyclical. Masai's pay-after-placement model buckled in the 2023 to 2024 hiring freeze. The opening is a role none of them is built for.

bet 01

Why it compounds

The placement flywheel

Each placement makes the next one easier. Outbound seeds it; referrals spin it.

Cold outreach gets the first placements. Those students ship real work, which becomes case studies and warm founder referrals, which make every next placement faster and cheaper. By months 5 and 6 the flywheel carries roughly a third of volume on its own.

The hardest question, answered

Why NIAT talent can credibly do this

An FDE is part engineer, part product operator, part customer strategist. NIAT already builds the engineering half. We add the customer half and a hard proof gate.

The engineering half comes from an AI-native, project-based, build-first curriculum refreshed every six months, with an OpenAI Academy collaboration and hands-on agentic tooling.

The claim stays narrow, which is why it holds: not that a tier-2 fresher is a senior FDE, but that a screened junior who has demonstrably shipped an agentic build is exactly what a Series A startup or a GCC AI team needs for last-mile work, at a price the market cannot match. Not everyone clears the gate. That is the point.
